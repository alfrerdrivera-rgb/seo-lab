Best GPS Tracker For Dogs

Published: June 2, 2026

Quick answer: The best GPS trackers for dogs combine real-time location updates, geofencing alerts, and a waterproof design — look for monthly subscription costs under $10.

What to look for

Accuracy depends on update frequency — every 2-10 seconds is ideal for real-time tracking. Battery life ranges from 1 to 7 days depending on tracking mode. Look for IP67 waterproofing minimum and check the monthly subscription cost — most require a cellular plan.

Key features worth paying for

Geofencing lets you set a safe zone and get an instant alert when your dog exits it. Activity tracking monitors daily movement. Some models include a light or sound feature for locating your dog in low visibility conditions.

What to avoid

Bluetooth-only trackers work within 30-100 feet of your phone — useless if your dog escapes. For real GPS tracking, you need a cellular model with a SIM card and a data plan.

Final thoughts

A GPS tracker gives genuine peace of mind for escape-prone breeds. Compare subscription costs carefully — over two years the plan usually costs more than the device itself.
