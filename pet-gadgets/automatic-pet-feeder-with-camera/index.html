Automatic Pet Feeder With Camera

Published: June 4, 2026

Quick answer: Camera feeders combine scheduled meals with live video, two-way audio, and sometimes treat dispensing — letting you monitor and talk to your pet at every meal from anywhere.

What's included

Most models offer 1080p live video, night vision, two-way audio via built-in speaker and microphone, and smartphone app control of feeding schedules. Some include a treat launcher separate from the main food hopper for additional interaction.

Privacy and connectivity

Camera feeders stream video via Wi-Fi to the cloud or directly to your phone. Check the privacy policy for end-to-end encryption and whether video is stored locally or in the cloud. Some brands have had security issues with internet-connected pet cameras — research before buying.

Dry food only

Like most automatic feeders, camera models are designed for dry kibble. The camera adds cost and interaction but doesn't change the dry-food-only limitation. If your pet needs wet food, a separate camera alongside a wet food feeder is often more reliable.

Final thoughts

A camera feeder is worth it if you want to observe mealtime behavior and interact remotely. For pure feeding reliability, a simpler non-camera feeder is often more durable and easier to maintain.
