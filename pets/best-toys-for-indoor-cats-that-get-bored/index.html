Best Toys For Indoor Cats That Get Bored

Published: March 24, 2026

Quick answer: Indoor cats can become bored without enough stimulation, leading to unwanted behaviors. Choosing the right toys helps keep them active and mentally engaged.

Why this happens

Toys that mimic prey, such as feather wands and moving objects, activate your cat’s hunting instincts. These provide both physical and mental exercise. Tip: rotate toys regularly to keep them interesting.

What you can do

Puzzle toys and treat dispensers encourage problem-solving and slow feeding. They can keep your cat entertained even when you’re not around. Tip: start with simple puzzles before increasing difficulty.

When to worry

Some cats respond well to automated toys that move unpredictably. However, too much passive play can reduce engagement over time. Tip: combine interactive play with solo toys for balance.

Final thoughts

The best toys are those that match your cat’s personality and energy level. With the right mix, indoor cats can stay active, curious, and happy.
