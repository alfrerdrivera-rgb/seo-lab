How To Help A Constipated Cat At Home?

Published: March 21, 2026

Quick answer: Constipation in cats is a common issue that can cause discomfort and changes in behavior. While mild cases can be managed at home, it’s important to recognize when professional help is needed.

Why this happens

Cats can become constipated due to dehydration, lack of fiber, or hair ingestion from grooming. Stress and low activity can also slow digestion. Tip: ensure your cat has constant access to fresh water.

What you can do

Simple home remedies include adding wet food, increasing water intake, and encouraging movement through play. Some owners use small amounts of pumpkin for fiber support. Tip: introduce dietary changes gradually to avoid stomach upset.

When to worry

If your cat strains, cries in the litter box, or hasn’t passed stool for more than two days, it may be serious. Blockages or chronic issues require veterinary attention. Tip: never force treatment if your cat shows pain or distress.

Final thoughts

Mild constipation can often be managed at home, but ongoing or severe symptoms should not be ignored. Early care helps prevent complications and keeps your cat comfortable.
